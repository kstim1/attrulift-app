Attribution Models
How credit is distributed across marketing touchpoints
LAST TOUCH Simple
Last-Touch Attribution
Assigns 100% of the conversion credit to the final touchpoint before the conversion occurred. Easy to understand and implement, but systematically over-credits bottom-funnel channels like branded search and retargeting ‚Äî channels that often close deals but didn't create the intent.
Best for Short, simple sales cycles with few touchpoints. Direct response campaigns.
‚ö† Can lead to over-investment in retargeting at the expense of awareness channels that drive top-of-funnel demand.
FIRST TOUCH Simple
First-Touch Attribution
Assigns 100% of the credit to the first touchpoint in the conversion journey ‚Äî the channel that first introduced the prospect to the brand. Useful for understanding which channels are most effective at generating new demand, but ignores everything that happens in the nurture and close phases.
Best for Understanding awareness and demand generation effectiveness. Brand building measurement.
‚ö† Understates the value of nurture and conversion channels. Not useful for optimizing full-funnel spend.
LINEAR Balanced
Linear Attribution
Distributes credit equally across all touchpoints in the conversion path. A simple but fair starting point for multi-touch attribution ‚Äî it avoids the extremes of first- and last-touch and gives a more realistic picture of how multiple channels work together to drive conversions.
Best for Multi-channel B2B campaigns with longer sales cycles. A good default when you don't have enough data to support data-driven models.
‚úì Attrulift's recommended default model for most SMB use cases.
TIME DECAY Intermediate
Time-Decay Attribution
Gives more credit to touchpoints that occurred closer to the conversion, with credit decreasing exponentially as you go further back. Based on the assumption that more recent interactions had more influence on the final decision. Uses a configurable half-life (default: 7 days) to control the rate of decay.
Best for Short sales cycles and promotional campaigns where recency matters. Flash sales, time-limited offers.
‚ö† Can under-credit awareness campaigns that drove initial interest weeks before conversion.

Identity Resolution & Match Methods
How Attrulift connects conversions to ad exposures
CLICK ID High Confidence
Click ID Matching
Deterministic matching using platform-generated click identifiers ‚Äî gclid (Google), fbclid (Meta), li_fat_id (LinkedIn). When the same click ID appears in both your conversion data and exposure data, the match is exact. Confidence: 100%.
‚úì Most reliable method. Requires click ID to be captured at conversion time (e.g. passed through landing page URL parameters into your CRM).
COMPANY NAME Medium Confidence
Company Name Matching
Probabilistic matching using company name fuzzy similarity (bigram algorithm). Particularly powerful for B2B attribution using LinkedIn exposure data, where company-level targeting information is available. A similarity score above 80% triggers a match. Adding geo agreement raises confidence further. Confidence: 70‚Äì95%.
Ideal for LinkedIn campaigns with company targeting. B2B with CRM data including account names.
GEO + TIME Low Confidence
Geo + Time Window Matching
Population-level matching based on geography and time proximity. If an ad was shown in New York and a conversion happened in New York within the attribution window, a probabilistic association is made. The confidence score decreases as the gap between exposure and conversion grows. Confidence: 25‚Äì50%.
‚ö† High false-positive risk in densely targeted geos. Use the confidence threshold setting to control inclusion.
TIMESTAMP ONLY Very Low Confidence
Timestamp-Only Matching
Last-resort matching when no geo, company name, or click ID is available. Matches any exposure that occurred within the attribution window purely based on time overlap. This is purely correlational ‚Äî it establishes that an ad ran and a conversion happened in the same period, nothing more. Confidence: 10‚Äì22%.
‚ö† Excluded by default (confidence threshold 0.6). Lower your threshold to 0.3 to include these. Treat results with significant caution.

Key Metrics Explained
What each number means and how to use it
Attributed Conversions
The number of conversion events that were successfully matched to at least one ad exposure within the attribution window. Does not double-count ‚Äî each conversion is counted once regardless of how many touchpoints it matched to.
Attributed Revenue
The sum of conversion values distributed across matched channels using the selected attribution model. In multi-touch models (linear, time-decay), a single conversion's value may be split across multiple channels. Total attributed revenue will equal total conversion value for matched records.
Avg. Conversion Value
Total attributed revenue divided by number of attributed conversions. Calculated only on conversions with a non-zero value (excludes lead form fills and other $0 conversion events). Useful for understanding the quality of conversions each channel drives, not just the volume.
Cost per Conversion (CPC)
Total ad spend divided by attributed conversions for a given channel. A lower CPC indicates more efficient spend. Compare across channels to find where your budget is working hardest. Note: this is a correlation-based metric ‚Äî it doesn't prove causality.
ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)
Attributed revenue divided by ad spend. A ROAS of 3x means every $1 spent generated $3 in attributed revenue. Industry benchmarks vary widely by channel and industry ‚Äî B2B typically sees lower ROAS than B2C due to longer sales cycles and higher deal values.
Match Rate
The percentage of total conversion records that were matched to at least one ad exposure. A low match rate (under 40%) usually means your identity fields are sparse ‚Äî prioritize capturing click IDs at conversion time or ensuring company names are consistent between your CRM and ad platform exports.
Avg. Touchpoints
The average number of ad exposures matched to each conversion. Higher values indicate longer, more complex purchase journeys and suggest multi-touch attribution models will give you more accurate results than single-touch models.
Confidence Score
A 0‚Äì100% score indicating how reliable a given match is. Calculated based on match method (click ID = 100%, company name = 70‚Äì95%, geo+time = 25‚Äì50%, timestamp only = 10‚Äì22%). The average confidence score for a channel reflects the overall data quality of that channel's attribution.

Frequently Asked Questions
Common questions about attribution methodology
Why does my attributed revenue add up to more than my total revenue? ‚ñæ
In multi-touch attribution models (linear, time-decay), credit is split across touchpoints ‚Äî so a single $10,000 conversion might be split as $5,000 to LinkedIn and $5,000 to Google. The total attributed revenue across all channels will still equal total matched conversion value. If it exceeds that, check for duplicate records in your conversion upload.
What's the difference between attribution and incrementality? ‚ñæ
Attribution answers "which channels were present in the path to conversion?" Incrementality answers "which channels actually caused the conversion ‚Äî would it have happened anyway without the ad?" Attribution is correlational. Incrementality requires a controlled experiment (holdout test or geo lift study). Attrulift does attribution. For incrementality measurement, you'd need to run lift studies through each platform or use a clean room setup.
My match rate is low. How do I improve it? ‚ñæ
The most impactful thing you can do is capture click IDs at conversion time. For Google and Meta, this means passing the gclid/fbclid URL parameter through your landing page into your CRM or purchase record. For LinkedIn B2B campaigns, ensure your CRM captures company name consistently with how LinkedIn reports it. For geo matching, make sure both files use the same geo format (city name, state, etc.).
Which attribution model should I use? ‚ñæ
Linear is the best default for most SMBs running multi-channel campaigns. It's transparent, easy to explain to stakeholders, and avoids the systematic biases of single-touch models. Use last-touch if you're primarily optimizing a direct-response campaign with a short funnel. Use time-decay if you have a short sales cycle and want to weight recent intent signals more heavily. Avoid over-rotating on model choice early ‚Äî data quality (match rate, coverage) matters more than model sophistication at your scale.
How should I think about geo+time and timestamp-only matches? ‚ñæ
Treat them as directional signals, not proof. A geo+time match means an ad ran in the same market during the same period as a conversion ‚Äî suggestive, but not causal. Timestamp-only matches are even weaker: they only establish temporal overlap. These are most useful in aggregate (e.g., "LinkedIn was active in all the geos where we saw conversion spikes") rather than at the individual record level. Never present these to stakeholders as definitive attribution.
What does the attribution window setting do? ‚ñæ
The attribution window defines how far back in time Attrulift looks for ad exposures that could be associated with a conversion. A 30-day window means any ad exposure in the 30 days before a conversion is considered a candidate touchpoint. Shorter windows (7 days) suit fast sales cycles and direct response. Longer windows (60‚Äì90 days) suit B2B with longer consideration periods. Setting the window too wide creates noise; too narrow misses real influence.
Can I trust ROAS numbers from Attrulift? ‚ñæ
Use them directionally. Attrulift's ROAS figures are based on correlated attribution ‚Äî they show you which channels appear alongside conversions, not which channels caused them. They're more reliable when match confidence is high (click ID or company name matching) and less reliable when based on geo or timestamp matching. A channel with a 4x ROAS and 90% average confidence is a much stronger signal than a channel with 4x ROAS and 30% average confidence.
How is Attrulift different from the attribution reporting inside Google Ads or Meta? ‚ñæ
Platform-native attribution is inherently biased toward that platform ‚Äî Google Ads attribution will always show Google performing well because it only sees its own touchpoints. Attrulift is channel-neutral: it ingests data from all your platforms simultaneously and applies a consistent model across all of them. This gives you a cross-channel view that no single platform can provide.
